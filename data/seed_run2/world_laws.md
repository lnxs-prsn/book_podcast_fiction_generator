## DOCUMENT 1: WORLD BIBLE

### Premise
In the Megalopolis, “power” is the act of mining language data—treating text as ore that can be refined, processed, and sold. The core resource is a clean **Data Scroll**: a text corpus that has been tokenized, stop‑word‑removed, stemmed, and lemmatized, yielding a compact, tradable bundle of keywords and morphemes. Failure is a **Scroll Explosion**: when a scroll is improperly cleaned, the unchecked noise (stop‑words, repeated tokens, non‑standard orthography) detonates, literally blowing up the market stalls that hold it, harming merchants and destabilizing trade.

### The 5 Stages

| Stage | Source Domain Concept      | [Genre] Metaphor           | Core Truth                                                         | Failure Mode                                                    |
|-------|-----------------------------|----------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------|
| Stage 1 | Tokenization               | Glyph‑Slicing             | “Each word is a thin shard that must be separated.”                | Tokens jam together, causing a rolling tide of unreadable a copy.|
| Stage 2 | Stop‑word removal          | Noise‑Shaping             | “Cut away the chatter that hides worth.”                           | Stale words clog the market, making scrolls untouchable.       |
| Stage 3 | Stemming & Lemmatization   | Root‑Forging              | “Reduce words to their breath of life, the root.”                  | Morphological chaos spreads, rendering entire shelves inert.    |
| Stage 4 | Statistical language model | Probability Gauges        | “Patterns in words predict the future of trade.”                   | Bad gauges mislead traders, causing market crashes.            |
| Stage 5 | Transformer architecture   | Wind‑Wardens              | “A wind‑warden listens to all and points to the crucial line.”   | A mis‑wired wind‑warden releases gibberish, flooding the city. |

### [World] as [Source Domain]

- **Glyph‑Slicer (Tokenizer)**: A skilled scribe splits raw lore into individual runes, exactly as a tokenizer splits text into tokens.
- **Noise‑Shaper (Stop‑word remover)**: A merchant trims away filler slogans and superfluous chatter from trade contracts to reveal value.
- **Root‑Forge (Stemmer/Lemmatizer)**: A blacksmith reduces forged blades to steel cores, mirroring how stemming/lemmatization removes affixes to reveal a word's root.
- **Probability Gauge (Statistical Model)**: A clerk uses a weather‑meter built from word‑frequency dust to forecast market tides.
- **Wind‑Warden (Transformer)**: An automated storm‑watcher distributes attention across an entire storm‑cloud of words, highlighting the most consequential gusts.

### Power System Rules

1. **Precision Rule** – Only properly tokenized text can be processed by a scroll‑printer. A malformed token stream renders the printer inert, and the city’s market stalls go silent.
2. **Noise Rule** – Any stop‑word that remains in a scroll carries a “noise point.” Excess noise accumulates until a boulder of redundant text explodes in the market, killing prices and merchants.
3. **Root Rule** – Words that cannot be lemmatized (i.e., do not map to a known root) become “rogue glyphs” that attract mischief, causing adjacent scrolls to flip unpredictably.
4. **Attention Rule** – A wind‑warden may only focus on sections of a scroll that have been cleaned. Uncleaned sections spawn “dead zones” where the wind‑warden goes blind, spamming useless outputs.
