## DOCUMENT 3: STYLE CONTRACT

### Voice

- **Descriptive realism**. Show the grit of the Megalopolis: neon, rust, the hiss of data streams. — Not: “The city was beautiful like a shimmering dream.”
- **Skeptical humor**. Alex’s dry quips about “data dragons” must feel earned. — Not: “He laughed, then cursed the whole world.”
- **First‑person confusion**. Alex’s internal voice should fluctuate between familiar folk terms and raw data thinking. — Not: “Alex suddenly understood the universe.”

### Jargon Introduction Rule

1. **Experience the problem** — Alex splinters a scroll, the pieces clamor like fire.
2. **Feel the stakes** — The market stalls ignite as corrupted words explode.
3. **Receive the pattern** — A mentor whispers, “Every word must be a single shard.”
4. **Attach the label** — The term “tokenization” enters only after Alex realizes the shards logic.
5. **Use naturally thereafter** — The word surfaces in conversation; no further definition needed.

### Failure Dramatization

- **Immediate physical consequence.** A scroll turns into a fireball that knocks over merchants.
- **Emotional cost.** Alex cripples a mother who relies on scrap trades, feeling deep regret.
- **Social stakes.** The guild of Cleaners whispers judgments; Alex’s apprenticeship is threatened.

### What Never Happens

- A character lectures another using jargon they both just learned.
- A technique/skill/action succeeds perfectly the first time (no learning).
- Alex is rewarded with a crystal heart for winning a marketplace bet.
- Alex is granted a throne by the Wind‑Warden.
