## DOCUMENT 3: STYLE CONTRACT

### Voice
- **Declarative and short.** "The switch hurt. But the lesson was wrong." Not: "Although the physical sensation of the switch was painful, Amina realized that the underlying pedagogical assumption was fundamentally flawed."
- **Physical before philosophical.** Bodily sensation grounds every abstraction.
- **Negative space.** What is *not* said matters. The gap between "The Core was beautiful" and "It was also a bottleneck" is the teaching moment.

### Jargon Introduction Rule
1. **Experience the problem** (Amina's fever: skin says cold, blood fights, sweat arrives late)
2. **Feel the stakes** (she almost dies)
3. **Receive the pattern** (the jade slip: "two parts, both doing their jobs, had misaligned their timing")
4. **Attach the label** — *only after the reader already grasps the concept*
5. **Use naturally thereafter** — no scare quotes, no re-explanation

### Failure Dramatization
- **Immediate physical consequence.** Not "the system failed" but "her arm believed the cup was elsewhere, her eyes insisted it was here."
- **Emotional cost.** Shame of wrong understanding, not just pain of wrong action.
- **Social stakes.** Others watching who don't understand, who judge by old rules.

### What Never Happens
- A character lectures another using jargon they both just learned
- A technique succeeds perfectly the first time (no learning)
- The narrator explains what a character feels before the character feels it
- "As you know, Bob" exposition
