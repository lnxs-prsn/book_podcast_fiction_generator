## DOCUMENT 2: CONCEPT CURRICULUM

### Teaching Principles
- **Concrete before abstract.** Every concept arrives as bodily experience first, label second.
- **Failure teaches.** Characters survive wrong understanding, then revise. No lecture without prior pain.
- **One hard concept per arc.** Easy concepts (decentralization, SPOF) can pair with hard ones. Never two hard concepts together.
- **Repetition without re-teaching.** Once "backpressure" is learned, later chapters use it naturally, don't re-explain.

### Difficulty Scale Applied
- **2-3:** Decentralization, SPOF, backpressure — intuitive from daily life
- **4-5:** Asynchrony, protocol, blocking, stale reads — requires holding two processes in mind
- **6-7:** Consensus, choreography, quorum, partition — counter-intuitive, challenge identity
- **7+:** Eventual consistency as identity, CAP theorem as philosophy — only after all prior earned
