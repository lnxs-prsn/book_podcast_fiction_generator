--- ACTIVE VOCABULARY ---

**decentralization**
- The body is not one thing.
- Different parts of the body (skin, muscles, lungs, gut, head) can be in different states and have different needs.
- Actions by one part can conflict with or ignore the needs of another part.
- Grandmother imposes a "one will, one purpose" idea that fails to address the body's distributed nature.

--- TERMS LEARNED BUT NOT YET OWNED ---

**asynchrony**
- Amina’s body parts act out of sync. Her skin is hot, she shivers, her lungs gasp, her gut churns, sweat arrives late.
- The grandmother’s imposed will on Amina's head is disconnected from the body's immediate, uncoordinated responses.

**single point of failure**
- Grandmother’s approach, focusing solely on the head/forehead with the stone, is a single point of failure. When it doesn't work, there's no other mechanism to address Amina's widespread distress.
- Amina’s own lack of understanding of her body’s distributed nature makes *her* a potential single point of failure in coordinating her own health.

--- TERMS INTRODUCED THIS ARC ---

Arc 1 target terms: decentralization, asynchrony, single point of failure.
- **Decentralization**: Introduced through Amina’s fever symptoms – her body parts warring with each other. Grandmother’s "One will. One purpose." maxim directly contrasts this.
- **Asynchrony**: Demonstrated by the conflicting sensations (hot skin, shivering, sweat arriving late) and the disconnected actions of different body parts.
- **Single point of failure**: Grandmother’s stone method fails because it's focused on one point without addressing the distributed problem.

--- ACTIVE FORESHADOWING ---

- Amina's internal conflict suggests future challenges in coordinating different aspects of her cultivation.
- The grandmother's "one will, one purpose" philosophy acts as a direct counterpoint to the emerging concept of decentralization and will likely be revisited as a flawed paradigm.

--- PROTAGONIST LENS ---

Amina starts without any systems vocabulary. She perceives problems
through sensation and intuition. Her first arc teaches her that her body
is not one thing — it is a distributed system that must be coordinated,
not commanded. She experiences the failure of a centralized will (grandmother's approach) to manage the body's distributed and asynchronous nature.
