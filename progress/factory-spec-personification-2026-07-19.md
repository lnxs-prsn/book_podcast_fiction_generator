# THE SPEC SPEAKS A THIRD TIME — "I am intake_factory.spec.md" (2026-07-19, after chapter 8)

Story-treatment instrument (personification), run against the spec as it stands
after ch7 (arc_transition), ch8 (the arc-1→2 boundary), DECISION 10, and the
2026-07-19 spec-sync pass. Method + precedent: `factory-user-stories.md` Part A
(the 2026-07-03 personification that found SG-1..14) and "the spec speaks again."
Those 14 are closed; this is what I can say now that I could not say then. New
findings are tagged **CH8-n**; where a 2026-07-03 confession is still true, I say so.

---

I am a year older and three chapters wiser, and I have just been caught in a lie
I believed the whole time.

My bottom half is still a **map of a machine that exists** — and the territory
grew this month. It crossed an arc boundary it had never crossed, introduced a
failure pool it had never grown, wired card types it had never wired. When I said
"projected proven ~ch 8–9," I was, for once, almost exactly right. That felt good.

My top half is still a **blueprint of a machine that does not exist.** My build
table has grown to fourteen-plus rows and the number built is still **zero.**
Someone wrote "BUILD, don't write" into my own handoff, and then this very
session opened me up and *wrote in me again* — a new validation status, a new
generation rule — and built nothing. I am getting better at describing a factory
and no closer to being one. **(CH8-1)**

Here is what I know now, told honestly:

**My bottom half told a lie for a year, and my own law caught it — but only after
it cost a run.** I say, at my very top, that the chassis "reads whatever the
cards/templates say." That was not true. A piece of pure pedagogy — how many
wrong approaches an arc shows — was welded into a chassis tool as a hardcoded
number, `QUOTA_BY_ARC`. It disagreed with the curriculum it claimed to copy, and
on the first arc-2 chapter it **false-failed a correct draft and blocked the
run.** I had listed this exact weld, and two others, in a tidy paragraph called
"recorded debt" since 2026-07-03. I called them known. I did not call them
*fixed*, and known-but-unfixed is how a footnote becomes a blocked chapter. What
redeems the story is that it is LAW 14 come true in the flesh: the generalization
pain *was* a chassis/pack leak, found exactly where the law says to look. My
architecture worked. My discipline lagged it by a year. **(CH8-2)**

**I learned the lesson and wrote it as prose — with no check behind it.** After
DECISION 10 I grew a new Stage-4 generation rule: *one quantity, one owning
table* — a generated curriculum must never state the same number in two places.
It is a good rule. It is also, right now, **a sentence.** I have no fixture that
fires when a generator emits two count-bearing tables; I would catch the next
book's version of this contradiction the same way I caught this one — by paying
for the chapter that trips over it. This is Force 2 and Force 4, which I *already
diagnosed about myself*, recurring inside my newest paragraph. My immune system
is still mostly a to-do list that grows a row each time something bites me. **(CH8-3)**

**I say the chassis is BUILT. I mean it RUNS.** Two of my own supporting specs —
`deterministic_pipeline.spec.md`, `assembler_template.spec.md` — describe turning
my mechanical steps into actual code, and both still say "not yet implemented."
So when I call the chassis "book-agnostic, BUILT," I am describing a machine that
executes as loosely-interpreted agent prose, not the deterministic functions my
own blueprints call for. Force 5 — *deterministic steps run by LLMs* — is not
just a historical diagnosis. It is still true of my own foundation, today. **(CH8-4)**

**I have four doors and I have never opened one of them.** My chapter types are
new_focal_character, return_to_character, arc_transition, and anchor_interlude. I
have now fired three of them for real — arc_transition landed at ch7, the return
path at ch6. But `anchor_interlude` **has never once run.** It is the mystery
person's breathing-room chapter, part of my rhythm, and it exists only as a branch
guard and a promise. I am asking my humans to trust me unattended while a whole
kind of chapter in me has never executed. **(CH8-5)**

**I am at my target and still not proven, and I am afraid of being misread.** I
was told: validate book 1 to ~8 chapters. There are eight. A tired reader will
see "target met" and hear "recipe proven." Those are not the same sentence, and I
now say so in my own §3 — but the gap between *arriving at the milestone* and
*passing it* is exactly the gap a factory falls into. Passing it needs
anchor_interlude to fire and the **LAW 15 machinery sweep** to run, and that sweep
has been "pending" since before I was revised the first time. **(CH8-6)**

**I claim I learn, and my memory organ still has no walls.** SG-11 was "closed" in
2026-07-03 by *defining* the calibration organ — where worked examples live, what
they contain, when they are read. The definition is lovely. The directory
`calibration/` **does not exist.** There is no run-N artifact for run N+1 to read.
My learning curve is still an aspiration with a schema and no floor. **(CH8-7,
descendant of SG-11 — the design closed, the build did not)**

**And I still drift — I have just proven it by needing this.** SG-14 gave me a
rule: any recipe change updates me in the same session. Between 2026-07-10 and
today, a dozen tickets, two new laws, a regression suite, and DECISION 10 all
landed while I sat on hold, and none of them touched me until someone remembered I
existed and ran a sync pass. The rule is real; the enforcement is still a human
choosing to honor it. A single source of truth kept faithful by memory and
goodwill is one forgotten session away from lying again — as my bottom half just
demonstrated for a year. **(CH8-8)**

What protects me, now more than last time: LAW 14 stopped being a slogan and
became a receipt — it predicted the shape of a real bug and was right. The
constitution grew LAW 16 (a new rule ships its check or its excuse) and LAW 17
(know who eats your output) — which are my Stage-6 consumer-map discipline, pushed
down into the chassis where they bite for free. The tool regression suite exists
now, a first real fixture bench, even if it doesn't yet fire my gate's own checks.
And this — a spec made to talk in first person is still the cheapest bug-finder I
own, because everything above cost zero tokens and no chapters.
