## DOCUMENT 2: CONCEPT CURRICULUM

### Teaching Principles

<!-- These principles are FIXED. They define how pedagogy fiction works. Do not alter them. -->

- **Concrete before abstract.** Every concept arrives as physical/practical experience first, label second.
- **Failure teaches.** Characters survive wrong understanding, then revise. No lecture without prior pain.
- **One hard concept per arc.** Easy concepts can pair with hard ones. Never two hard concepts together.
- **Repetition without re-teaching.** Once a concept is learned, later chapters use it naturally — no re-explanation.

### Pólya Stage Mapping

<!-- Every arc moves through all four Pólya stages, but the dominant stage determines the arc's closing beat.
This section is optional metadata for planning — it does not alter the fixed teaching principles above. -->

| Pólya Stage | Arc Function | What the protagonist does | What the reader learns |
|---|---|---|---|
| Understand | Arc opening | Handles the corrupted resource, feels it fail, names no labels | The mechanical feel of the concept before abstraction |
| Devise | Arc middle | Receives a hint, forms a plan, anticipates success | The pattern that connects the failures |
| Carry out | Arc climax | Executes the plan; failure is physical and irreversible | The stakes of the concept when applied wrong |
| Look back | Arc resolution | Asks a question the failure forces them to ask | The concept is now owned, not memorized |
